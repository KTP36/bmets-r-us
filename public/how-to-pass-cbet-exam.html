Your Complete CBET Exam Preparation Guide

The CBET (Certified Biomedical Equipment Technician) exam is challenging, but with the right strategy and dedicated study time, you can pass on your first attempt. This guide provides a proven framework used by thousands of successful candidates.

📊 Exam Readiness: CBET is a challenging exam. Consistent practice with structured question sets can improve confidence, speed, and overall readiness.

CBET Exam Format & Content

Exam Overview:

  • Duration: 3.5 hours
  • Number of Questions: 150 multiple-choice questions
  • Pass Score: Approximately 70% (105 points)
  • Cost: $300 (AAMI)
  • Format: Computer-based testing (CBT)

Content Breakdown:

  • Electrical Safety & Fundamentals (15%)
  • Patient Monitoring Systems (15%)
  • Therapeutic Equipment (20%)
  • Ventilators & Respiratory (15%)
  • Cardiac & Hemodynamic Equipment (15%)
  • Quality Control & Maintenance (10%)
  • Troubleshooting & Service (10%)

8-12 Week CBET Study Timeline

Weeks 1-2: Foundation Building

Review basic electrical concepts, safety standards, and exam format. Read through study materials for 1-2 hours daily. Take baseline practice questions to identify weak areas.

Weeks 3-6: Deep Dive Study

Spend 70% of study time on practice questions. Focus on high-weighted topics (Therapeutic Equipment, Ventilators, Monitoring Systems). Review missed questions daily. Join study group or find accountability partner.

Weeks 7-9: Intensive Practice

Take full-length timed practice exams weekly. Review all previous mistakes. Study harder-tier questions to exceed the passing threshold. Aim for 75%+ on practice exams.

Weeks 10-12: Final Review & Exam Prep

Continue timed full-length exams. Focus on remaining weak areas. Review test-taking strategy and time management. Take the last 7 days light—review notes, do light practice, rest well.

Top 10 Strategies to Pass the CBET Exam

  • Use Practice Questions as Your Primary Study Tool — Spend 60-70% of study time on practice questions. The exam tests application, not just memorization.
  • Study Unit by Unit, Not the Whole Exam — Master one equipment type or concept before moving to the next.
  • Understand Why Answers Are Correct or Wrong — Read explanations for both correct and incorrect options.
  • Track Your Progress — Keep notes on weaknesses so you can focus review sessions.
  • Take Timed Practice Exams — Speed matters. Practice with a timer to build confidence under pressure.
  • Review Missed Questions Within 24 Hours — While the concept is still fresh in your mind.
  • Study Real-World Scenarios — Understand how equipment functions in clinical settings, not just theoretical specs.
  • Join a Study Group — Explaining concepts to others reinforces your understanding.
  • Get Adequate Sleep During Study Period — Sleep is when your brain consolidates learning.
  • Take the Exam When You're Scoring 75%+ on Practice Tests — Don't attempt the real exam until you're consistently above passing threshold.

Day-Before & Day-Of Exam Tips

The Day Before:

  • Do light review only—avoid cramming new material
  • Get 8+ hours of sleep
  • Review test center location and arrival time
  • Prepare all required identification documents

Exam Day:

  • Arrive 15 minutes early
  • Eat a full breakfast with protein and complex carbs
  • Skip excessive caffeine
  • Stay calm—you've prepared for this
  • Read questions carefully before answering
  • Skip difficult questions and return to them later
  • Manage time: ~1.4 minutes per question

Common CBET Exam Mistakes to Avoid

  • Over-relying on memorization — CBET tests application and troubleshooting, not facts.
  • Not doing enough practice questions — This is the #1 reason for failing.
  • Rushing through questions — Careful reading saves points.
  • Studying isolated facts instead of systems — Understand how equipment components work together.
  • Ignoring weak areas — Double down on topics where you score lowest.
  • Taking the exam too early — Wait until practice scores consistently meet/exceed 70%.
