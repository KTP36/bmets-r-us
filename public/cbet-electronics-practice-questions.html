Voltage, Current, and Resistance Questions

1. What does voltage represent in a circuit?

  • A. Flow of electrons
  • B. Electrical potential
  • C. Opposition to current
  • D. Stored energy
Answer: B. Electrical potential

Voltage is the electrical potential or pressure that pushes current through a circuit. It helps drive electron flow.

2. What is current?

  • A. A type of resistance
  • B. Stored electrical pressure
  • C. Flow of electrons
  • D. Circuit interruption
Answer: C. Flow of electrons

Current refers to the movement of electrical charge through a circuit. This is one of the core ideas behind Ohm’s Law.

3. What does resistance do in a circuit?

  • A. Increases electron storage
  • B. Opposes current flow
  • C. Converts AC to DC
  • D. Measures voltage
Answer: B. Opposes current flow

Resistance limits or opposes the flow of current. If resistance goes up and voltage stays the same, current goes down.

4. If voltage increases while resistance stays the same, what happens to current?

  • A. It decreases
  • B. It stays zero
  • C. It increases
  • D. It becomes AC
Answer: C. It increases

According to Ohm’s Law, current increases when voltage increases if resistance stays constant.

5. If resistance increases while voltage stays the same, what happens to current?

  • A. It increases
  • B. It decreases
  • C. It becomes unstable
  • D. It turns into voltage
Answer: B. It decreases

Current is inversely related to resistance. More resistance means less current flow for the same voltage.

6. Which law connects voltage, current, and resistance?

  • A. Pascal’s Law
  • B. Faraday’s Law
  • C. Ohm’s Law
  • D. Boyle’s Law
Answer: C. Ohm’s Law

Ohm’s Law is one of the most important electronics relationships for CBET prep and troubleshooting.

Quick thinking shortcut:
  • Voltage increases: current increases if resistance stays the same.
  • Resistance increases: current decreases if voltage stays the same.
  • No voltage: there is no electrical push for current flow.

AC vs DC Questions

7. Which type of current flows in one direction?

  • A. AC
  • B. DC
  • C. Pulsed current only
  • D. Bipolar current
Answer: B. DC

Direct current flows in one direction. Many internal device circuits rely on DC. Review AC vs DC current for a deeper explanation.

8. Which type of current changes direction periodically?

  • A. DC
  • B. Static current
  • C. AC
  • D. Battery current only
Answer: C. AC

Alternating current changes direction on a regular cycle. This is the type of power commonly associated with wall outlets.

9. Why do many electronic devices convert AC to DC internally?

  • A. DC is easier to see
  • B. Many internal circuits require one-direction current flow
  • C. AC cannot be measured
  • D. DC always uses less energy
Answer: B. Many internal circuits require one-direction current flow

Devices may receive AC from the wall but convert it to DC because many internal electronics require stable current in one direction.

  • C. Brain Anatomy Quiz
  • D. RN Practice Questions
Answer: A. AC vs DC Current

What to notice first:

Devices may receive AC from the wall, but many internal circuits need DC. That means AC-to-DC conversion is a major power supply concept.

Diodes, Capacitors, and Rectifiers Questions

11. What is the main function of a diode?

  • A. Store energy
  • B. Allow current to flow mainly in one direction
  • C. Increase resistance automatically
  • D. Measure power
Answer: B. Allow current to flow mainly in one direction

A diode acts like a one-way path for current. Learn more on the diode guide.

12. What does a capacitor do?

  • A. Converts AC to DC
  • B. Blocks all voltage
  • C. Stores and releases electrical energy
  • D. Creates resistance only
Answer: C. Stores and releases electrical energy

Capacitors are used in filtering, timing, and energy storage. Review what a capacitor does.

13. What does a rectifier do?

  • A. Increases current by default
  • B. Converts AC to DC
  • C. Measures voltage
  • D. Reduces resistance
Answer: B. Converts AC to DC

Rectifiers are key parts of many power supplies. Review what a rectifier does for more detail.

14. Which component is especially important in power supply conversion from AC to DC?

  • A. Capacitor only
  • B. Rectifier
  • C. Fuse only
  • D. Sensor lead
Answer: B. Rectifier

A rectifier is one of the key components used to convert AC input into DC output.

15. Which component can help smooth electrical output in a circuit?

  • A. Capacitor
  • B. Diode only
  • C. Switch only
  • D. Connector
Answer: A. Capacitor

Capacitors often help smooth output in circuits and power supplies by storing and releasing energy.

16. A failed diode may most directly affect which function?

  • A. Airway pressure reading
  • B. Current direction control
  • C. Screen brightness only
  • D. Oxygen saturation
Answer: B. Current direction control

Since diodes mainly control the direction of current flow, a failed diode can interfere with that behavior.

Component thinking:
  • Diode: controls direction of current flow.
  • Capacitor: stores energy and helps smooth output.
  • Rectifier: converts AC into DC.

Power Supply and Troubleshooting Questions

17. Why are power supply concepts important in biomedical equipment?

  • A. Devices do not need stable power
  • B. Equipment depends on correct electrical conversion and delivery
  • C. Only batteries matter
  • D. Power has no effect on circuit behavior
Answer: B. Equipment depends on correct electrical conversion and delivery

Power supplies help convert and regulate electrical energy so devices can function properly and safely.

18. If a device receives wall AC power but its internal circuits need DC, what system must work properly?

  • A. Audio speaker
  • B. AC to DC conversion path
  • C. Plastic casing
  • D. Keyboard membrane
Answer: B. AC to DC conversion path

This conversion path often involves rectifiers and other components that support usable internal DC power.

19. If a capacitor fails in a circuit, what kind of problem might you expect?

  • A. Timing or smoothing issues
  • B. Anatomical labeling error
  • C. Oxygen sensor calibration only
  • D. No possible effect
Answer: A. Timing or smoothing issues

Capacitors are often involved in smoothing and timing, so failure can affect those functions.

20. Why is understanding component function useful in troubleshooting?

  • A. It helps predict what may fail or behave abnormally
  • B. It removes the need for test equipment
  • C. It replaces all service manuals
  • D. It is only useful for anatomy study
Answer: A. It helps predict what may fail or behave abnormally

Knowing what a component does helps you reason through symptoms, failures, and abnormal circuit behavior.

21. A circuit with too much resistance for a given voltage is likely to have what problem?

  • A. Excessive current flow
  • B. Reduced current flow
  • C. Unlimited power
  • D. Automatic rectification
Answer: B. Reduced current flow

Higher resistance reduces current flow when voltage remains unchanged.

What to notice first:

Power supply questions often ask whether you understand the path from wall power to usable internal power. Think input, conversion, smoothing, regulation, and output.

How to Think Through CBET Electronics Questions

Instead of memorizing every electronics fact separately, slow down and ask what the question is really testing. Most circuit questions can be broken into a few simple ideas.

  • What is pushing the current? Voltage.
  • What is flowing? Current.
  • What is limiting it? Resistance.
  • What component is controlling behavior? Diode, capacitor, rectifier, switch, or supply stage.
  • Where could failure occur? Power input, conversion, smoothing, regulation, or output.
What to notice first:

Every circuit problem becomes easier when you identify the relationship being tested.

Real-World Troubleshooting Thinking

CBET electronics questions are easier when you connect them to actual equipment behavior. Biomedical equipment depends on correct power delivery and predictable component function.

  • No power: check the supply path.
  • AC is present but device is dead: consider the AC-to-DC conversion stage.
  • Unstable output: consider capacitors, filtering, or regulation.
  • Current direction problem: consider diode behavior.
  • Reduced current: consider increased resistance or poor connection.
What to notice first:

Most equipment failures become easier to reason through when you follow the power path first.
