Medical Terminology Guide

Learn how medical terms are built from prefixes, suffixes, and root words so healthcare language starts to make sense.

What Is Medical Terminology?

Medical terminology is the language used in healthcare to describe body systems, symptoms, conditions, procedures, and treatments.

At first, medical words can look overwhelming. But most terms are built from smaller word parts.

  • Prefix — comes at the beginning of a word
  • Root word — gives the main meaning
  • Suffix — comes at the end of a word
What to notice first:

Do not try to memorize the whole word first. Break it into parts.

Why Medical Terminology Matters

Medical terminology helps students and healthcare workers understand what a word means even if they have never seen that exact word before.

This matters in nursing, TEAS prep, anatomy, patient care, documentation, and communication with the healthcare team.

Once you understand the word parts, you can start recognizing patterns instead of guessing.

How to Break Down a Medical Term

A simple way to read medical terms is to start at the end, then look at the beginning, then identify the root.

  1. Look at the suffix first
  2. Look at the prefix
  3. Find the root word
  4. Put the meaning together
What to notice first:

The suffix often tells you the condition, procedure, or process.

Example:

Gastritis = gastr means stomach + itis means inflammation.

So gastritis means inflammation of the stomach.

Common Medical Prefixes

Prefixes usually tell you direction, speed, amount, time, or location.

PrefixMeaningExample
Hyper-Above normal or highHypertension
Hypo-Below normal or lowHypoglycemia
Brady-SlowBradycardia
Tachy-FastTachycardia
Neo-NewNeonatal
Pre-BeforePreoperative
Post-AfterPostoperative
Sub-Under or belowSubcutaneous
What to notice first:

If the word begins with hyper or hypo, look for whether something is high or low.

Common Medical Suffixes

Suffixes often tell you what is happening in the word. They may describe inflammation, pain, removal, study, or a blood condition.

SuffixMeaningExample
-itisInflammationArthritis
-ectomySurgical removalAppendectomy
-ologyStudy ofCardiology
-algiaPainNeuralgia
-emiaBlood conditionAnemia
-megalyEnlargementCardiomegaly
-scopyVisual examinationEndoscopy
-pathyDiseaseNeuropathy
What to notice first:

If a word ends in -itis, think inflammation. If it ends in -ectomy, think removal.

Common Root Words

Root words usually point to the body part or system involved.

What to notice first:

The root tells you what body system or body part the term is about.

Examples of Medical Terms Broken Down

  • Hypertension — hyper means high, tension refers to pressure
  • Bradycardia — brady means slow, cardia refers to the heart
  • Tachycardia — tachy means fast, cardia refers to the heart
  • Gastritis — gastr means stomach, itis means inflammation
  • Dermatitis — derm means skin, itis means inflammation
  • Appendectomy — appendix + ectomy means removal of the appendix
  • Hypoglycemia — hypo means low, glyc refers to sugar, emia means blood condition

Common Mistakes Beginners Make

  • Trying to memorize the whole word instead of breaking it down
  • Ignoring the suffix
  • Confusing hyper and hypo
  • Forgetting that root words often refer to body systems
  • Assuming every medical word has only one part
What to notice first:

Start with the part you recognize. One familiar part can unlock the meaning of the whole word.

How This Helps With TEAS, Nursing, and Healthcare Study

Medical terminology shows up across healthcare learning.

In TEAS prep, it can help with reading science questions more confidently.

In nursing school, it helps with understanding diagnoses, documentation, procedures, and patient education.

In anatomy and physiology, it helps connect terms to body systems.

Medical terminology is not just vocabulary. It is a way to understand what the word is telling you.
