What to Notice First

These cues help you recognize schematic symbols quickly instead of guessing.

Resistor: Look for the zig-zag pattern. That is the key visual cue.
Capacitor: Look for two parallel plates. That is the clue that energy is being stored.
Diode: Look for the arrow or triangle-like direction clue pointing toward a line.
Switch: Look for a break or gap in the path. Open means the circuit is interrupted.
Ground: Look for the ground symbol or reference point. It shows the circuit reference.
Junction: A dot usually means the wires connect. Crossing lines without a dot usually means they do not connect.
