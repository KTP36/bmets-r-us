How to Read an EKG (Beginner Guide)

Learn the basics of EKG interpretation including rhythm recognition, waveforms, and key patterns.

Important Note

This guide is for educational purposes only. It is designed to help students and learners understand EKG basics. It does not provide medical certification or professional training.

What is an EKG?

An electrocardiogram (EKG) records the electrical activity of the heart. It helps identify heart rhythms and detect abnormalities.

Basic EKG Components

  • P Wave: Atrial contraction
  • QRS Complex: Ventricular contraction
  • T Wave: Ventricular recovery

Simple Step-by-Step Approach

  1. Check the heart rate (slow, normal, or fast)
  2. Look for P waves
  3. Assess rhythm regularity
  4. Evaluate QRS width
  5. Match pattern to known rhythms

Common Rhythms

  • Normal Sinus Rhythm
  • Atrial Fibrillation
  • Ventricular Tachycardia
  • Asystole
