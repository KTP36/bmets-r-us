What Is a Diode?

A diode is an electronic component that allows current to flow mainly in one direction while blocking current in the opposite direction. This guide explains forward bias, reverse bias, rectifiers, protection circuits, and why diodes matter in CBET electronics and medical equipment.

Quick Answer

A diode allows current to flow mainly in one direction and blocks it in the opposite direction.

A diode acts like a one-way valve for electrical current. This one-way behavior makes diodes important in rectifiers, power supplies, signal circuits, and protection circuits.

Forward direction → Current flows    |    Reverse direction → Current blocked
What to notice first: If a CBET question asks what component controls current direction, the answer is often diode.

Why Diodes Matter

Diodes matter because many circuits need current to move in a controlled direction. Without that control, a circuit may not convert power correctly, protect sensitive components, or handle signals the way it should.

  • They allow current to flow mainly in one direction.
  • They are used in rectifiers to convert AC into DC.
  • They can help protect circuits from reverse polarity.
  • They support signal and switching behavior in many electronic circuits.
CBET clue: If the question mentions one-way current flow, rectification, or reverse polarity protection, think diode.

How a Diode Works

A diode has two terminals: an anode and a cathode. In basic terms, current can flow through the diode when it is connected in the correct direction. When it is connected in the opposite direction, it blocks current under normal conditions.

Anode

The side where conventional current enters when the diode is forward-biased.

Cathode

The side where conventional current leaves when the diode is forward-biased.

Forward Bias

The diode is connected in a direction that allows current to flow.

Reverse Bias

The diode is connected in a direction that blocks current flow under normal conditions.

Simple way to remember it: diode = one-way current path.

Forward Bias vs Reverse Bias

Diode Condition What Happens Memory Hook
Forward Bias The diode conducts current when voltage is applied in the correct direction. Forward means flow.
Reverse Bias The diode blocks current under normal conditions. Reverse means restrict.
Failed Shorted The diode may conduct in both directions when it should not. Too much flow.
Failed Open The diode may not conduct when it should. No flow.

Diodes in Rectifiers

One of the most important uses of diodes is rectification. A rectifier uses diodes to convert AC into DC. This matters because wall power is AC, while many internal electronic circuits require DC.

AC input → Diodes / Rectifier → DC output

A bridge rectifier commonly uses four diodes. This is a high-value CBET electronics concept because it connects diodes, AC vs DC current, power supplies, and medical equipment troubleshooting.

Why Diodes Matter in Medical Equipment

Diodes are used in medical equipment power supplies, signal circuits, and protection circuits. They are essential in devices that require controlled current flow and stable operation.

  • Power supplies: diodes help convert AC into DC.
  • Protection circuits: diodes can help protect against reverse polarity or voltage spikes.
  • Signal processing: diodes can help shape or control signal flow.
  • Charging circuits: diodes may help control current direction during charging behavior.
Biomedical equipment connection: If a device needs DC power internally, diodes may be part of the rectifier stage that helps create that DC power.

Diode vs Rectifier vs Capacitor

Component Main Job CBET Memory Hook
Diode Allows current to flow mainly in one direction. One-way current path.
Rectifier Uses diodes to convert AC into DC. AC-to-DC conversion.
Capacitor Stores and releases electrical energy. Energy storage and smoothing.
Resistor Opposes current flow. Limits current.

Basic Troubleshooting Insight

A diode can fail open or shorted. If it fails open, it may block current when it should conduct. If it fails shorted, it may allow current in both directions when it should not.

Common clue: A failed diode may affect current direction control, rectifier behavior, reverse polarity protection, or power supply output.

Common CBET-Style Questions

1. What is the main function of a diode?

A. Store energy   B. Allow current to flow mainly in one direction   C. Measure pressure   D. Increase oxygen flow

Answer: B. Allow current to flow mainly in one direction
A diode is used as a one-way path for electrical current.
2. What does forward bias usually allow?

A. Current flow   B. Complete current blockage   C. No circuit action   D. Stored patient data

Answer: A. Current flow
Forward bias means the diode is connected in a direction that allows current to flow.
3. What does reverse bias usually do?

A. Allows normal current flow   B. Blocks current under normal conditions   C. Stores energy   D. Smooths ripple directly

Answer: B. Blocks current under normal conditions
Reverse bias means the diode is connected in the blocking direction.
4. What circuit uses diodes to convert AC into DC?

A. Rectifier   B. Oxygen blender   C. Thermometer probe   D. Display bezel

Answer: A. Rectifier
Rectifiers use diodes to convert AC power into DC power.
5. A bridge rectifier commonly uses how many diodes?

A. One   B. Two   C. Four   D. Eighty

Answer: C. Four
A common bridge rectifier arrangement uses four diodes.
