Brain Anatomy Practice Questions

  1. Which part of the brain is responsible for balance and coordination?
    • A. Cerebrum
    • B. Cerebellum
    • C. Medulla oblongata
    • D. Thalamus
    Answer: B. Cerebellum
  2. Which part of the brain controls many involuntary functions like breathing and heart rate?
    • A. Medulla oblongata
    • B. Frontal lobe
    • C. Parietal lobe
    • D. Occipital lobe
    Answer: A. Medulla oblongata
  3. Which lobe is primarily associated with vision?
    • A. Temporal lobe
    • B. Frontal lobe
    • C. Occipital lobe
    • D. Parietal lobe
    Answer: C. Occipital lobe
  4. Which lobe is involved in decision-making and problem-solving?
    • A. Frontal lobe
    • B. Temporal lobe
    • C. Occipital lobe
    • D. Cerebellum
    Answer: A. Frontal lobe
  5. Which structure acts as a relay center for sensory information?
    • A. Thalamus
    • B. Hypothalamus
    • C. Pons
    • D. Pituitary gland
    Answer: A. Thalamus
  6. Which lobe is important for hearing and language comprehension?
    • A. Temporal lobe
    • B. Occipital lobe
    • C. Parietal lobe
    • D. Frontal lobe
    Answer: A. Temporal lobe
  7. The largest part of the brain is the:
    • A. Pons
    • B. Cerebellum
    • C. Cerebrum
    • D. Medulla
    Answer: C. Cerebrum
  8. Which brain structure helps regulate body temperature and hunger?
    • A. Thalamus
    • B. Hypothalamus
    • C. Cerebellum
    • D. Corpus callosum
    Answer: B. Hypothalamus

Why Practice Brain Anatomy?

Brain anatomy is a high-value topic for anatomy study, nursing prep, and medical vocabulary review. Repetition improves recall and test confidence.
