Before You Start

A multimeter is one of the most important beginner tools because it helps answer practical troubleshooting questions.

Simple pattern: Check voltage to see if power is present, continuity to see if the path is complete, and resistance to evaluate opposition to current flow.
Safety reminder: Resistance and continuity checks should be done with power off and stored energy safely discharged.

What to Notice First

These cues help you connect the quiz questions to real multimeter troubleshooting.

Questions 4 and 19: Meter lead placement matters. Black usually goes in COM, and the red lead must match the measurement type.
Questions 5 and 6: Resistance checks are different from voltage checks. Resistance should be checked with power off.
Questions 7 through 9: Continuity helps determine whether a path is complete or open.
Questions 11 and 12: Batteries are DC sources. Wall outlets are AC sources.
Questions 16 through 18: Capacitors can store energy. They must be handled carefully and safely discharged before testing.
Question 20: The safest move is not guessing. Stop and confirm the correct setting and safety steps.
