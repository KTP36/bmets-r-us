Welcome to Module 1

Medical terminology is the language of healthcare. Medical assistants use it while reviewing charts, documenting information, communicating with clinicians, explaining instructions, scheduling procedures, and supporting patients.

You do not need to memorize every medical word. The goal is to recognize common word parts and use them to determine what an unfamiliar term probably means.
MedSkillBuilder Learning Strategy

Decode first. Memorize second.

When you see a difficult term, look at the suffix first, then find the root, and finally add the prefix. This turns one long word into smaller clues.

By the end of this module, you should be able to:

Break medical words into prefixes, roots, and suffixes.
Recognize common terms used in patient care and documentation.
Interpret directional, positional, and anatomical language.
Understand frequently used healthcare abbreviations.
Use context clues to decode unfamiliar medical terms.
Complete terminology practice with at least 80% accuracy.

Why This Matters

Imagine a chart says a patient has tachypnea, hypertension, and hypoglycemia. Understanding the word parts helps you quickly recognize rapid breathing, high blood pressure, and low blood sugar.

That vocabulary affects communication, documentation, patient safety, and your ability to understand the rest of a medical assistant curriculum.
