Frequently Asked Questions

What is the CRES exam?

The CRES (Certified Radiology Equipment Specialist) exam is administered by the International Certification Commission for Clinical Engineering and Biomedical Technology (ICC). It certifies professionals who maintain, repair, and manage diagnostic imaging equipment.

What topics does the CRES exam cover?

The CRES exam covers imaging physics, X-ray and fluoroscopy systems, CT and MRI equipment, ultrasound systems, radiation safety, preventive maintenance, and regulatory compliance including ACR and Joint Commission standards.

How many questions are on the CRES exam?

The CRES exam consists of approximately 150 multiple-choice questions. The exam tests both theoretical knowledge and practical application of radiology equipment principles.

How do I prepare for the CRES exam?

Effective CRES prep includes reviewing imaging physics fundamentals, studying equipment-specific maintenance protocols, practicing with sample questions, and focusing on radiation safety and regulatory compliance topics.

Study Tips for the CRES Exam
  • Master imaging physics first: Questions on X-ray production, kV/mA relationships, and image quality are foundational — get these solid before moving on to equipment specifics.
  • Know your modalities: Be able to distinguish how CT, MRI, ultrasound, and fluoroscopy work at a systems level, including their key components and failure modes.
  • Radiation safety is always on the exam: ALARA principles, dose limits, and shielding calculations appear consistently — these are quick points to pick up with focused review.
  • Review preventive maintenance protocols: The CRES heavily tests PM schedules, quality control tests, and acceptance testing procedures for each modality.
  • Study regulatory standards: Know ACR technical standards, Joint Commission requirements, and state radiation control program basics.
