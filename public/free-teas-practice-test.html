How to Use This Free TEAS Practice Test Effectively

1. Take your first attempt honestly

Do not pause to look up answers. Your first attempt should show your real starting point.

2. Review every missed question

Do not stop at the score. A missed question tells you exactly what needs attention.

3. Sort mistakes by pattern

Look for repeated issues. Are you missing math setup? Science vocabulary? Reading inference? Grammar rules?

4. Practice weak sections first

It feels easier to practice what you already know, but your score improves faster when you target weak areas.

5. Repeat with timing

Once accuracy improves, add timed practice so you can build speed and confidence.

What to notice first:

Improvement comes from review, not just repetition.

Free TEAS Practice Test vs TEAS Practice Questions

A practice test gives you a broader view of your readiness. Practice questions help you drill specific skills.

You need both.

  • Use a practice test to find weak sections and measure readiness.
  • Use practice questions to improve specific topics and fix repeated mistakes.

Common TEAS Practice Test Mistakes

  • Only looking at the final score: The score matters, but the missed questions matter more.
  • Skipping review: Taking another test without reviewing the last one repeats the same mistakes.
  • Ignoring timing: Accuracy is important, but pacing also matters.
  • Overstudying one section: A balanced score requires all four sections.
  • Memorizing instead of recognizing: You need to understand what the question is asking.
What to notice first:

The questions you miss more than once are your highest-value study targets.

TEAS Reading Practice Tips

Reading questions often test whether you can find evidence in the passage.

  • Read the question carefully
  • Look for the main idea
  • Use evidence from the passage
  • Avoid answers that sound true but are not supported

TEAS Math Practice Tips

Math mistakes often happen because the setup is wrong before the calculation starts.

  • Identify what the question is asking for
  • Write the setup first
  • Watch units carefully
  • Practice fractions, percentages, ratios, and conversions

TEAS Science Practice Tips

Science questions often test systems, processes, and relationships.

  • Review anatomy and physiology by body system
  • Connect structure to function
  • Practice common medical terminology
  • Look for cause and effect

Strengthen science vocabulary with the Medical Terminology Guide, Top 100 Medical Terms, and Medical Prefix and Suffix Practice.

TEAS English Practice Tips

English questions usually test a rule. Find the rule and the answer becomes easier.

  • Check subject-verb agreement
  • Watch punctuation
  • Look for sentence fragments
  • Look for run-on sentences
  • Use context clues for vocabulary

Simple TEAS Study Plan After Your Practice Test

  1. Day 1: Take a practice test or mixed practice set.
  2. Day 2: Review missed reading and English questions.
  3. Day 3: Review missed math questions.
  4. Day 4: Review missed science questions.
  5. Day 5: Practice your weakest section.
  6. Day 6: Take a short timed mixed set.
  7. Day 7: Review and reset your plan.
What to notice first:

Your next study session should be based on what you missed, not what feels easiest.
