How to Improve TEAS Scores

1. Baseline first

Take an initial set to see your current strengths and weaknesses.

2. Target weak sections

Spend extra time where accuracy is lowest, not where you already score well.

3. Review mistakes in detail

Understanding why an answer is correct usually improves retention faster than memorizing.

4. Repeat over multiple sessions

Score consistency across sessions is a better readiness signal than a single high score.
