TEAS Practice Questions

Free practice for reading, math, science, and English with a focus on real improvement—not memorization.

Why Practice Questions Matter

Most students do not struggle because they do not know the material.

They struggle because they cannot recognize what the question is actually asking.

What to notice first:

TEAS questions test recognition and interpretation under pressure—not just recall.

If science terminology slows you down, review: Medical Terminology Guide or Top 100 Medical Terms.

How to Use TEAS Practice Questions

  • Take small sets daily
  • Review every missed question
  • Track repeat mistakes
  • Focus on weak areas
What to notice first:

Most improvement happens during review—not during the first attempt.

TEAS Subjects

  • Reading: main idea, inference, structure
  • Math: setup, percentages, conversions
  • Science: systems and relationships
  • English: grammar and clarity

Need help with science vocabulary? Practice: prefix and suffix training

How to Improve Faster

1. Start with a baseline

Identify weak areas first.

2. Focus your effort

Do not spend time on what you already know.

3. Fix mistakes immediately

This is where most gains happen.

4. Repeat consistently

Consistency beats cramming.

What to notice first:

The fastest improvement comes from fixing patterns—not doing more questions.

Common Mistakes

  • Skipping review
  • Rushing questions
  • Ignoring timing
  • Overstudying one section
